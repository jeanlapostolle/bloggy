Title: Metropolitan Sleeper 
Date: 2026-04-26 9:47
Category: Poésie
Summary: Texte dans la langue de David Tennant à propos des personnes SDF.
Tags: poésie

Every day I see the metropolitan sleeper. He is here on the floor of the dock.
Everybody knows he is here. The view, the smell and sometimes the sound are something we can’t forget.
But for everyone, it’s normal. That’s not why they forget him, they just don’t want to talk about him or just talk to him.
The metropolitan sleeper is now a nobody.

But has it always been like that? Has the metropolitan sleeper always been ignored by everyone?

Of course not, the metropolitan sleeper was once a little child, with a beautiful family.
He grew up like everybody, with a loving mother and a strict dad.
At school, he was not bad, but he was not very good either.
Every Saturday, he played Football with his friend.
When he grew up, he got married and had a child. He was a strict loving dad.
He had been working a long time for a coal mine. 

The mines have been closing for ten years now. 
The savings fled, and the marriage went on the rocks.
The metropolitan man has lost everything. His loving parents, his friends, his wife, his child, his life…

The metropolitan sleeper doesn’t sleep anymore.

I will not see him every morning on the dock’s floor. 
I will not smell his atrocious odor.

But I haven’t forgotten him like everybody, 
And tomorrow possibly, the metropolitan sleeper will be me.
